Requirements + Pathways

Traditional College Programs (The “Deep Foundation”)

Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT)

Tuition Estimate: ~$64,310 per year; however, families earning under $200,000 typically attend tuition-free as of the 2025-2026 academic year.

Program Strengths: World-leading research in AI and systems, rigorous engineering foundation, and unparalleled industry networking.

Admission Requirements: Extremely competitive; 25th-75th percentile SAT scores typically range from 1510 to 1570.

Arizona State University (ASU)

Tuition Estimate: ~$12,177 (In-state) to ~$33,139 (Out-of-state).

Program Strengths: Top-ranked for innovation; strong industry partnerships in the "Silicon Desert" (Phoenix metro).

Admission Requirements: Minimum 3.0 GPA in competency courses, or top 25% of high school class, or an SAT of 1120 (1180 for non-residents).

Specialized Bootcamps (The "Fast Track")

General Assembly (GA)

Cost: Approximately $16,450.

Length: 12 weeks (Full-time) or 24 weeks (Part-time).

Job Placement Support: Comprehensive career coaching, resume workshops, and an 84% placement rate within the first 6 months of graduation.

Certifications & Professional Programs (The "Skill Stacker")

Google Career Certificates

Cost: ~$39/month (via Coursera subscription).

Time to Complete: 3–6 months at 10 hours/week.

Career Relevance: Entry-level readiness for UX Design or IT Support; recognized by a consortium of over 150 employers.

CompTIA (A+ / Security+)

Cost: ~$250–$400 per exam voucher.

Time to Complete: Self-paced; typically 2–4 months of study.

Career Relevance: The gold standard for foundational IT support and cybersecurity roles.
